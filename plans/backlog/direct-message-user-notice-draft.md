# Direct Message User Notice Draft

## Japanese Abstract

This is a draft for users before direct message launch. It explains the feature at a high level. The
final legal text must be reviewed before publication.

## Purpose

This document is a draft source for future user-facing notice pages, terms updates, and privacy
updates related to one-to-one direct messaging.

It is not final legal text. It is a product and engineering draft so that implementation work does
not start without a visible user communication plan.

## Draft User Notice

### Direct Message Feature

Our service may provide a one-to-one direct message feature between users.

This feature is intended for communication related to service use. It must not be used for illegal
acts, harassment, fraud, threats, spam, or other prohibited conduct.

### Data We Handle

When you use direct messages, we may handle data such as:

- sender and recipient account identifiers
- message metadata such as timestamps and technical identifiers
- message content
- device, session, and network-related security data where required for service operation, security,
  abuse prevention, or legal response

### Why We Handle This Data

We may use message-related data for:

- service delivery
- security and abuse prevention
- account protection
- incident response
- legal compliance
- response to valid requests from courts, regulators, or other authorized bodies

### Retention And Deletion

We may retain message-related records for a limited period that is necessary for service operation,
security, abuse handling, legal compliance, or dispute response.

The final retention period and deletion rules must be defined before launch and reflected in the
published privacy notice and terms.

### User Responsibilities

Users must not use direct messages to:

- violate law or third-party rights
- send malware or fraudulent links
- harass, threaten, or impersonate others
- distribute spam or unauthorized advertising

Violation may result in message restrictions, account restrictions, content preservation, or other
service measures.

### Legal And Disclosure Response

Where required by law, court order, or other valid legal process, we may preserve or disclose
message-related information.

The exact handling flow must be defined in internal operations before launch.

### Service Changes

We may change, suspend, or stop the direct message feature when required for safety, legal
compliance, maintenance, or service redesign.

## Required Follow-Up Before Publication

- legal review of the final wording
- final retention schedule
- final privacy notice language
- final terms language
- final disclosure response policy
